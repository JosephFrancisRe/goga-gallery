The origin

The origin of Green Valley Zoo began with a lifelong fascination for animals that I carried with me from a young age. Ever since I can remember, I’ve been captivated by the beauty, diversity, and mystery of wildlife from around the globe. After graduating high school, I knew I didn’t want to wait any longer to turn that passion into something real. With a clear vision and relentless determination, I founded Green Valley Zoo with the goal of bringing the world’s most exotic and awe-inspiring animals into one place where people of all ages could learn, explore, and connect with nature. What started as a dream quickly became a reality, and today, Green Valley Zoo stands as a celebration of wildlife and a testament to the power of following your passion.

about the zoo

  • Founded Right After High School: Green Valley Zoo was established by a lifelong animal lover just after graduating high school, turning a childhood dream into reality.
  • Home to Over 150 Species: The zoo is home to more than 150 species of animals, including rare and exotic creatures from every continent.
  • Conservation First: Green Valley Zoo is dedicated to wildlife conservation, working with international programs to help protect endangered species.
  • Interactive Animal Encounters: Guests can get up close with animals through experiences like giraffe feeding, reptile handling, and behind-the-scenes tours.
  • Eco-Friendly Design: Built with sustainability in mind, the zoo uses solar panels, rainwater collection systems, and recycled materials throughout the park.
  • Education for All Ages: The zoo offers daily keeper talks, educational programs, and seasonal camps to inspire future generations of wildlife advocates.
  • Award-Winning Exhibits: Several enclosures have won awards for their naturalistic design, including the Tropical Rainforest Dome and the Savannah Plains exhibit.
  • Volunteer & Internship Opportunities: Local students and animal enthusiasts can gain hands-on experience through the zoo’s robust volunteer and internship programs.
  • Annual Visitors: Green Valley Zoo welcomes over 250,000 visitors annually, making it one of the top family attractions in the region.
  • A Growing Vision: The zoo continues to expand, with plans to open a new Arctic Habitat and Nocturnal House in the coming year.
