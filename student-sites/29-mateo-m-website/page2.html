Gameplay Review

The main gameplay loop is to train your characters in career mode to win races for either the Team Trials game mode, or the more competitive Champions Meeting. To start lets go over career mode.

Career Mode

The base senario is called "URA Finale." The career is split into Junior, Classic, and Senior year. There are two turns in a month, early and late. For the first 12~ turns you start training for your debut race. This involves a bunch of intertwined systems like energy, the 5 main stats, skill points, training facilities, mood level, random events, friendshp training, and support cards. To start you have an energy bar with 100 max energy, and you start your career with it maxed.

Training

There are 5 stats coresponding to 5 training facilities. They are Speed, Stamina, Power, Guts, and Wits. Your stats start out depending on the character but hover around 80-110. The cap for all stats are set at 1200. The certain ranges corespond with letters,
  • G:0-99
  • F:100-199
  • E:200-299
  • D:300-399
  • C:400-599
  • B:600-799
  • A:800-999
  • S:1000-1200

Here's a look

When you train at a facility you consume energy, (exept when you train wits) to gain stats and skill points.

  • Speed Facility: Consumes 20~ energy to raise speed, also rasies a small amount of power and skill points.
  • Stamina Facility: Consumes 16~ energy to raise stamina, also rasies a small amount of guts and skill points.
  • Power Facility: Consumes 14~ energy to raise power, also rasies a small amount of stamina and skill points.
  • Guts Facility: Consumes 24~ energy to raise guts, also rasies a small amount of speed, power and skill points.
  • Wits Facility: Restores 5~ energy to raise wits, also rasies a small amount of speed and a medium amount of skill points.

Here's another look



When you're bellow about 40~ energy there starts to become a chance to fail that training. Failing any training but wits triggers an event where you lose 5 of the stat that corresponds with facility you failed training and get your mood level reduced by 1. Mood level comes in 5 tiers Awful, Bad, Normal, Good, Great. Mood can be increased in many ways, but manualy you can use a turn for recreation to raise mood level. To restore energy, one option is to take a turn to rest, which triggers an event usualy restores 50 energy with a small chance to restore 70 or 30, if you restore 30 there's also a small chance to reduce mood level by 1 and be inflicted with a negitive staus condition. To recover from a negitive status condition you can take a turn to go to the imfirmary, which restores 20 energy, and has a very high chance of removing the negitive status condition, but annoyingly the restoration is not guarented. (This later will be changed to be guarented.)

Race Mechanics

In races your character's stats, running style, track profeceiencies, skills, mood, and plain old luck all effect the races's outcome. Lets start with the stat's role in races, Speed affects your top speed, on all distance styles it tends to be the most important stat. Stamina is effectively an hp bar that gets consumed the longer the race is, and if you run out you get seriously slowed down, so it's important to have just enough depending on the track. Power affects acceleration, so how fast you reach your top speed, it also improves your navigation around other racers. Guts is just the worst. In the current version of the game it just acts as emergency reserve stamina. Wits detrmines the % chance that your skills proc, and reduces the likelyhood of a late start and being rushed. In races, late starts just makes you start later than other racers, but being rushed makes you consume way more stamina.

After the 12~ turns of training you have your debut race which is very easy but not guarented to win. From the 2nd turn after your debut race there will start to be races you can take a turn to compete in. There are 4 tiers of races called OP, G3, G2, and G1. These increase in prestige in the order listed, with G1 races being the hardest, and most rewarrding to win. Every race has differences like, length coming in 4 classifications, first sprint 1000m-1500m, then mile 1600m-1800m, then medium 2000m-2400m, then long 2500m-3200m. Each increasing track length encorages more stamina to get through. Track material as turf or dirt. Track condition as wet, or firm. Season as summer, spring, autumn, winter. Weather as clear, cloudy, rainy, or snowy. The sections of a track are important too. There's the starting gates, all tracks have at least 1 corner, some have more, there's straighaways, uphills, downhills, the final stretch, and the finish post.

Now for the running styles, Front Runners try to stay at the front for the whole race, they tend to set the pace of the race which determines the rate of stamina consumption for the rest of the pack, whilst themselfs tending to consume the most amount of stamina among the running styles. Pace Chasers try to stay just behind the front runners as to conserve a bit of stamina and try to pull ahead mid-late race. Late Surgers stay towards the back of the pack, conserving a decent amount of stamina, usualy making their move to the front in the final spurt. End Closers stay at the back of the pack, conserving a lot of stamina, and move to the front in the final spurt. Late Surgers End Closers tend to rely on late race acceleration and speed sklls to win, as even though they have a higher top speed, they start accelerating later.

The last major race mechanic are skills. Each character comes with a set of base skills and has a unique skill that they level up throughout their career. You win races, train at facilities, and go through events to earn skill points. Skill points are used to buy skills. You get skills from hints. You get hints from events. Most events are random, but there are many that can be pseudo controled to an extent.

Each character has a series of races they need to place in. These are called career goals. There are also scenario events that take place regardless of which character you are doing a run of. Events can buff stats, give skill points, raise mood, give skill hints.

Inspiration, Affinity, and Legacies

Let's go back a bit, before staring a career run you set up your legacies. Having good legacies can improve results at inspiration events. At the end of a career run a legacy is generated and can be used to inspire other characters. A characters whole legacy consists of 2 main-legacies, and 4 sub-legacies. Each of those 6 have what are called sparks. Sparks come in 1-3 star levels, and give bonuses durring inspiration events. There are blue sparks which give stat bonuses. There are green sparks which give weakened unique skill hints. There are red sparks which give track type, track material, or running style profeciency level-ups. Lastly, there are white sparks which give skill hints. There are 3 inspiration events in a career, 1 of which gives stats, track type, track material, and running style profecientcy bonuses, and a weakened unique skill. The other 2 give mostly the same, but give more stats, and now give skill hints. The type and star level of the sparks change what is given durring an inspiration event. Additionaly, legacy affinity is an important aspect of inspiration events too. Having high legacy affinity gives more bonuses in inspiration events. Each character has a affinity value assigned to all other characters. This means it is usualy optimal to use legacies from characters with high affinity values with the character you're trying to raise. Winning races that match a race one of your legacies won is another way to raise affinity, although by a small amount. Ephiphets are the final way to increase affinity. By completing certain acheivements in a career their legacy will obtain a list of ephiphets which raises affinity for the trainee using that lecagy. 3 times a day you can borrow a legacy from a friend.

Team Trials

Your objective is to raise 3 characters for all 4 track types on turf and for mile on dirt. This totals 15 characters. Every 2 real life hours you regain 1 RP. RP is used to challenge other trainers to races with your team against theirs. It's whoever out of the 6 gets first wins 1 point, first to 3 points wins. You also get a score that's increased by skills used, and placement durring the race. Each week your high score gets you put into Class 1-6, with 6 providing the most rewards, especialy maintaining your placement there.

Champions Meeting

Your objective is to raise 3 characters within a few weeks bassed on a given track. Then round 1 starts at a given date. You get 3 entries every day. Each entry lets you race 5 times. These races are against 2 other players, whoever gets first wins the race, the other 2 player lose. To qualify to round 2 you need to win 3/5 races in 1 entry. The same to qualify for round 3. In round 3 you just race once and get rewards bassed on the highest placement any of your characters acheived. You also receive a title that can be put next to your name depending on your results.
