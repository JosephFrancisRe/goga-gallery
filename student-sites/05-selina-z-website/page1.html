Owls

Multiple owl species, ranging from the well known barn owl to the lesser known long-eared owl.

The Barn Owl

It's scientific name, "Tyto Alba", means "white owl." This owl has a distinct heart-shaped face which aids in the owls hearing. The barn owl has asymmetrical ears, helping them pinpoint where a sound is coming from. This species normally mate for life unless one is killed, where the surviving owl may go out to form a new bond.

The Great Grey Owl

It's scientific name, "Strix Nebulosa", refers to an "ill-omened creature" in Roman myth, "craving human flesh" in Greek myth. "Nebulosa" refers to the owls plumage (feathers), having a cloudy/hazy/misty appearance. It is considered the largest owl by length, being found in the Northern Hemisphere in both Eastern and Western Hemispheres.

The Long-Eared Owl

It's scientific name, "Asio Otus", refers to "horned owl" (asio) in Latin and "small eared owl" (otus) in Greek. This species is apart of the larger family Strigidae, which contains most of the known owl species. Unlike other owls, they aren't strongly territorial, considered more so "nomadic" in times.
