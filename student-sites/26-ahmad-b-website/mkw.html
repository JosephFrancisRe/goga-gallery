Reasons on why I don't like Mario Kart World

Accessibility / Prices

Mario Kart World was released along with the Nintendo Switch 2, but it's only platform was the Switch 2. This problem made it so if you wanted to play Mario Kart World, you needed to buy a $450 console and buying Mario Kart World by itself is a staggering $80. The price alone caused backlash but the worse ending was the example and standard it set for other gaming companies like Sony and Microsoft to increase prices. So on top of being restricted to one console, it's price is not agreed upon by the community and sets a bad example.

Character Roster

(Disclamier: This is my opinion on the character selection, if you like it, that's fine.) The character roster for Mario Kart World is underwhelming to say the least. The roster doesn't have any unique appearences and just adds a bunch of enemies from the games and called it a day. No crossovers, no unique mario characters from the base ones (Mario, Luigi, Peach, Toad, etc.) and the roster is just not good as a whole.

Forced Vision

Mario Kart World introduces the brand new Intermission Tracks which is supposed to be you driving from one course to the next. On paper, it sounds cool! But actually doing it, yeah it doesn't cut it. Intermission Tracks became something that everyone despised because of how boring it was and the worse part was that when you actually got to the track, you played one lap of it. This somehow got even worse when they rolled out a patch for online (which is in the image above) that FORCED intermission tracks onto the Random option which was the only way to avoid it. This caused the brand new shiny Mario Kart's online to die in less than a month through it's lifespan.

In Conclusion, while Mario Kart World is a good game, it's flawed in player expression and instead opts for the route of shoving down a "new" mechanic into the game when the community doesn't want it and is a overall letdown in what the people were expecting after waiting 11 years for a new mainline Mario Kart game.
